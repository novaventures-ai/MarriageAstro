Ashtakoot Milan — The 36-Point Compatibility System

Ashtakoot Milan (also called Gun Milan) is the traditional Vedic method for evaluating marriage compatibility. It scores 8 dimensions (Kootas) based on the Moon's Nakshatra placement in each partner's chart:

Koota Points What It Measures
Varna1Spiritual and ego compatibility — Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra classification
Vashya2Mutual attraction and dominance — who influences whom in the relationship
Tara3Destiny and health compatibility — based on Nakshatra count (9 Taras cycle)
Yoni4Physical and sexual compatibility — 14 animal Yoni types with compatibility matrix
Graha Maitri5Mental and intellectual compatibility — friendship between Moon sign lords
Gana6Temperament matching — Deva (divine), Manushya (human), Rakshasa (demon)
Bhakoot7Emotional compatibility, love, prosperity, and family happiness
Nadi8Genetic and health compatibility — Vata, Pitta, Kapha constitution matching

Score interpretation: Below 18/36 — not recommended. 18-24 — acceptable. 25-30 — good match. 31-36 — excellent compatibility. Astro Marriage also applies Nadi Dosha, Bhakoot Dosha, and Gana Dosha cancellation rules that reduce false negatives by up to 40% compared to basic calculators.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Kundali matching (Gun Milan)?

Kundali matching, also known as Gun Milan or Ashtakoot Milan, is a Vedic astrology method that compares two birth charts to assess marriage compatibility. It scores 8 aspects (gunas) across 36 points covering temperament (Varna), dominance (Vashya), destiny (Tara), sexual compatibility (Yoni), mental compatibility (Graha Maitri), nature (Gana), health/wealth (Bhakoot), and genetic compatibility (Nadi).

How many points are needed for a good Kundali match?

In Ashtakoot Milan, a score of 18 or above out of 36 is considered acceptable for marriage. Scores above 24 are good, and above 30 is excellent. However, overall compatibility also depends on Mangal Dosha status, Nadi Dosha cancellation, synastry aspects, and timing alignment.

Can astrology predict marriage timing?

Vedic astrology uses multiple systems to predict favorable marriage periods. The Vimshottari Dasha system (120-year planetary cycle) identifies when Venus, the 7th house lord, or Jupiter are active. Jupiter-Saturn transits over marriage houses open windows. KP Astrology's sub-lord system can narrow timing to specific months. When 3+ methods converge, the prediction confidence is highest.
